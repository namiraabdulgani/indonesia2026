★ Borobudur Temple
Temple Structure Climb · Pre-booked Tickets
World's largest Buddhist temple — 9 terraces of relief carvings leading to the crown stupas. Use the electric buggy service at the entrance so the elderly don't walk the grounds unnecessarily. Infant in carrier for the climb; stroller at base. A licensed guide is worth the IDR 100–150K/hr to bring the Mahabharata panels alive.
🎟 Pre-booked online · mandatory 👟 Upanat sandals provided 👘 Shoulders + knees covered 🚌 Use buggy service at entrance ☀ Very hot by 10am — go early

Day 03
Culture, Shopping & Prambanan
Wed · Sep 23
09:30
★ Cultural Show
Keraton Yogyakarta · Wayang Golek
Wednesday's show is Wayang Golek (Menak Puppet) at 9:30am — arrive by 9:00 to settle the group. The shaded pavilions are ideal for elderly. Pram manageable in the outer courtyard. Infant can nap in carrier through the performance. Allow 1.5 hrs including the museum galleries.
🎭 Wayang Golek · Wed 9:30am 🏛 Opens 8:30am IDR 25K adult · IDR 20K child ~35 min from hotel
10:45
Shopping
Museum Sonobudoyo · Wayang & Art
Short walk from Keraton on the same square. The finest collection of Wayang Kulit puppets, traditional masks, and batik in Yogyakarta — and a shop where you can buy authenticated pieces. Watch artisans making puppets from buffalo hide before you choose. Best place in the city for meaningful art to bring home.
🎭 Authentic wayang kulit shop 🎨 Masks · batik · art 👁 Watch artisans at work
12:00
Lunch
Jalan Wijilan · Gudeg Bu Rini or Bu Lies
Two-minute walk from Keraton into Jogja's famous gudeg lane — both Bu Rini and Bu Lies are consistently praised for cleanliness, proper sit-down setting, and gudeg that isn't too sweet. Safe for sensitive stomachs. Young jackfruit stew with rice, egg, krecek, and opor ayam — Jogja's defining dish.
✅ Bule-belly safe · clean kitchen 🍚 Gudeg · Jogja's signature 👶 Mild & sweet — toddler-friendly
13:00
Coffee
Kopi Pakpos Nol Km · Rest Break
Right at Zero Kilometer Point — vintage Dutch-colonial aesthetic, great coffee, light snacks. Perfect pause before shopping while the infant feeds and the toddler resets. Rated 4.6 and very casual.
13:30
Shopping
Hamzah Batik · Beringharjo Market
Father-in-law relaxes in the AC van while the rest of the group shops. Hamzah Batik is air-conditioned with fixed prices — ideal for buying batik without haggling. For the market experience, Beringharjo's upper floors have the best batik selection at negotiable prices. Ask vendors: tulis (hand-drawn) vs cap (stamped).
🛍 Hamzah: AC · fixed price · quality cert 🖌 Beringharjo: tulis vs cap batik 💵 Cash only in market
14:30
Optional
Kota Gede · Silver District
If energy and time allow — Jl. Kemasan is lined with silver workshops. Tom's Silver and HS Silver have fixed prices and purity certificates. Watch artisans hand-beat silver into wayang figurines, bowls, and jewellery. Skip if the group is flagging — Prambanan is the priority.
⏱ Allow 45 min max 💍 Tom's Silver · HS Silver ✅ Skip if group is tired
15:15
★ Prambanan Temple
Prambanan · Late Afternoon Visit
Indonesia's largest Hindu temple complex — three towering spires of Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. Afternoon is cooler and less crowded than morning. Outer grounds are flat and stroller-friendly. Use the buggy service so the elderly don't have to walk the full perimeter. Hire a guide (IDR 100–150K/hr) — the Ramayana mythology is spectacular.
IDR 400K adult · IDR 225K child (3yo) 🚌 Buggy service available 🧳 Stroller-friendly outer grounds ☀ Cooler + quieter in afternoon
17:30
★ Sunset Dinner
Abhayagiri · Sumberwatu Heritage Resort
5-minute drive uphill from Prambanan. Perched on a hill with panoramic views of the temple spires and Mount Merapi — Prambanan at golden hour from above. Indonesian and international menu, elegant outdoor seating. Reserve a family-friendly table in advance. Open until 9pm Wed–Thu.
🌅 Sunset over Prambanan + Merapi 📞 Reserve ahead · ask for view table 🕐 Open Mon–Thu until 21:00
